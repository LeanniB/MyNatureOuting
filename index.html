Plant2Plate

Plant2Plate is a produce garden located on Univerity of Pittsbrugh campus. It is an initatice to connect the farm, the kitchen and the community through hands-on agricultural. Participants learn about planting and harvesting crops along with produce nutritional value. The Fresh produce grown is used within campus dining halls and donated to near by shelters. Urban gardens contibute to capturing carbon, reducing food miles and creating a sense of connection to the land. Other communities could replicate this Plant2Plate model. Acess to fresh produce can enchance food security along all while providing learning experiences. The success of this mission lies in its ability to integrate education, community and sustainability.

This bee house is a wooden structure that allowes for nesting sites within a garden that provides food, shelter and a safesapce for pollinators to thrive. The low-mainteance native plants that bloom within the garden attracts bees so that they can lay eggs in the small cavities in the bee house that gives protection. Having pollinator gardens provide bees a safe haven to raise their young and find food. These spaces faciliate essential pollination than enchances local ecosystems by promoting biodiveristy. Biodiverse ecosystems absorb emmissions and heat, reducing climate impact and improves air quality by filtering pollutants. A way to expand this idea is to raise awareness about the bee species along with their role and encourage other locations to create similar pollinator - friendly spaces.

A Rain Garden captures and manages stormwater runoff from surfaces to infiltrate into the soil. The plants and soil within the rain garden filter pollutants preventing them from eaching rivers, lakes and oceans. The removal of pollutants improves the over all quality of water. It reduces flooding in city areas such as strees and parking lots allowing rainwater to be absorbed by the ground instead of sewers. These gardens also provide food and shelter for butterflies, songbirds and other wildlife. Their presence engances biodiverity. They are both cost-effective and beautiful. The community can learn to understand the benefits of rain gardens by thinking of all the ways it does not only protect the infrastructure of land but the cleanliness of their surrounding bodies of water. The collaboration with community memebers that like hand-on nature work can help promote rain gardens within cities.
